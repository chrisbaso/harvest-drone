# Field Mapping and Boundaries

Field mapping turns a grower's application request into a safe mission plan. The map must tell the drone where to work and where not to work.

## Required map elements

- Field boundary and total acres.
- Staging area, access route, and crew position.
- Obstacles such as trees, poles, wires, buildings, pivots, ditches, roads, and water.
- Sensitive areas such as neighboring crops, houses, livestock, waterways, schools, and public roads.
- Buffer zones required by product label, customer policy, or site conditions.
- No-spray areas and planned turn zones.

## Boundary verification

Do not rely on an old boundary when crop stage, field access, or neighboring crops have changed. Operators should confirm the boundary with the customer, map source, or supervisor before flight.

## Operating standard

If the boundary is uncertain, do not spray the uncertain area. Pause, document the question, and get confirmation before continuing.
