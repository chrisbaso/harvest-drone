# Pre-Flight Checklist Discipline

The pre-flight checklist is not paperwork. It is the last operating control before the aircraft launches.

## Complete it for the specific mission

A checklist from yesterday or from a different field does not count. Conditions change: batteries, nozzles, firmware, payload, wind, people, obstacles, and field access may all be different.

## Minimum pre-flight review

- Aircraft structure, props, arms, landing gear, fasteners, and payload system.
- Battery condition, charge level, staging, and temperature.
- GroundLink/controller status, mission plan, geofence, and return-to-home behavior.
- Field boundaries, obstacles, roads, utilities, people, animals, and sensitive areas.
- Product, rate, carrier volume, tank mix, PPE, and label restrictions.
- Weather, drift risk, precipitation, temperature, and inversion risk.
- Emergency landing area and crew communication plan.

## Launch rule

If the checklist is incomplete, the mission does not launch. If a checklist item fails, the operator either corrects it, documents the correction, or escalates the blocker.
