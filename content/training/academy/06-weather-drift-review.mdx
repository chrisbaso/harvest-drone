# Weather and Drift Review

Weather review protects the crop, the operator, neighboring property, and the customer relationship. It is also part of the compliance record.

## Conditions to record

- Wind speed and direction.
- Gusts.
- Temperature.
- Humidity.
- Precipitation.
- Surface inversion risk.
- Forecast changes during the expected spray window.

## Drift decision

Wind direction matters as much as wind speed. A manageable wind can still be unacceptable if drift would move toward a sensitive crop, waterway, road, residence, or crew area.

## Stop-work rule

If wind exceeds the mission or product limit, if precipitation starts, or if inversion risk is present, the operator pauses the mission. Do not continue just because the job is almost done.

## Documentation

Weather values should be attached to the mission record. The record protects the customer and the operator when conditions are later questioned.
