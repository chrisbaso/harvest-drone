# Emergency Stop-Work Criteria

Operators must know exactly when to pause, land, isolate the area, and escalate.

## Stop immediately when

- Aircraft behavior is abnormal or control confidence is reduced.
- People, vehicles, animals, or equipment enter the operating area.
- Wind, gusts, rain, inversion risk, or visibility moves outside mission limits.
- Product label restrictions cannot be met.
- Payload, nozzle, pump, spreader, battery, or navigation systems are uncertain.
- A spill, drift complaint, collision, flyaway, lost link, or injury risk occurs.

## First response

Land safely when possible. Protect people first. Secure the aircraft and product. Notify the lead operator or supervisor. Preserve flight logs, weather conditions, photos, and operator notes.

## Documentation

Emergency events must be recorded in the platform. The record should include what happened, when it happened, who was notified, what corrective action was taken, and whether retraining or maintenance follow-up is required.
