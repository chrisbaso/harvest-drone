# Commercial Drone Operating Rules

Commercial agricultural drone work requires more than being able to fly the aircraft. The operator must meet aviation, pesticide, site safety, and company requirements before a mission is assigned.

## Core aviation requirements

- FAA Part 107 must be current for commercial UAS operations.
- Aircraft must be registered and compliant with Remote ID requirements.
- The pilot must follow airspace, altitude, visual line of sight, crew coordination, and waiver limitations.
- Site hazards and emergency landing areas must be reviewed before launch.

## Agricultural application requirements

Spray and spreading missions may require pesticide applicator licensing, product label compliance, buffer zones, weather restrictions, application records, and state-specific documentation.

## Stop-work authority

The operator must stop or delay work when any readiness gate is uncertain. Examples include expired credentials, aircraft maintenance items, missing pre-flight checklist, wind above limit, precipitation, people entering the work area, product mismatch, or unclear field boundaries.

## Required mindset

Safe commercial drone operations are repeatable. The pilot follows a checklist, documents the decision, and escalates exceptions instead of improvising in the field.
