# Harvest Drone Sales Playbook

## 1. How to read the dashboard

- Start with the `Hot` and `Warm` lead counts.
- Scan `Leads this week` and `Estimated acres represented` to understand current opportunity volume.
- Filter by state, crop, acreage range, and timing to find the most relevant calls for today.
- Open a lead detail to see score, reason codes, notes, timeline events, follow-up sequence, and the suggested follow-up summary.

## 2. What makes a Hot lead

- Large or meaningful acreage
- Strong crop fit, especially corn or corn/soy acres
- Near-term decision timing
- High openness to testing SOURCE or discussing a plan
- A reason-code mix that points to real human follow-up instead of just education

## 3. What Jake should do first

- Call Hot leads within 24 hours.
- Prioritize growers with near-term timing and large-acreage fit.
- Use the summary block to open with acreage, crop mix, timing, and the stated goal.
- Move the lead to `Contacted`, `Qualified`, `Proposal / Plan Sent`, `Won`, `Lost`, `Nurture`, or `Bad Fit` as soon as the next step is clear.
- Add internal notes immediately after each conversation so the dashboard stays useful.

## 4. Call script

"Hey [Name], this is [Rep] with Harvest Drone. You filled out the SOURCE fit check. I saw you're farming [acreage] acres of [crop] and looking at [goal]. I wanted to ask a couple quick questions about timing and whether you're considering a trial or broader application plan this season."

## 5. Objection handling

### "I'm skeptical."

Response:

"That makes sense. Most growers don't want hype. The goal is simply to see whether this fits your acres and timing, and whether a small test makes sense."

### "I already have a fertility program."

Response:

"This isn't meant to replace the whole plan without review. The question is whether SOURCE can complement your current program or fit into a targeted trial."

### "I'm just researching."

Response:

"Totally fair. We can send practical info and revisit when you're closer to application timing."

## 6. Follow-up templates

### Hot

- Day 0: "Your SOURCE fit check"
- Day 1: "Quick follow-up on your acres"
- Day 3: "Worth reviewing before application windows tighten"

### Warm

- Day 0: "What SOURCE is designed to do"
- Day 3: "When SOURCE may fit"
- Day 7: "Want to test it on select acres?"

### Nurture

- Day 0: "Thanks - here's more info"
- Day 5: "How growers think about nutrient efficiency"
- Day 14: "Planning ahead for next season"

### Low Fit

- Day 0: "Thanks - we'll keep you updated."

## 7. How to update lead status

- `New`: No human outreach yet.
- `Contacted`: First real outreach attempt happened.
- `Qualified`: Real sales conversation should continue.
- `Proposal / Plan Sent`: A plan, recommendation, or next-step document went out.
- `Won`: Revenue confirmed.
- `Lost`: Opportunity closed without revenue.
- `Nurture`: Still valuable, but not urgent.
- `Bad Fit`: Not worth immediate sales pressure.

## 8. How to track won/lost revenue

- Use `Revenue status` to mark `projected`, `won`, or `lost`.
- Add `Estimated value` when the team has a realistic planning number.
- Add `Actual revenue` once revenue is confirmed.
- Keep `Estimated acres` updated if the initial acreage midpoint changes after a real conversation.
- Add internal notes for what was sold, over how many acres, and why the deal was won or lost.
